# Health Pilot v0.5.0 UI Specification

## Objective

Implement the first production-quality Home screen for Health Pilot.

This release establishes the core interaction model:

**Mission First.**

The UI must follow all Design Principles, UI Principles, Decision Principles, and Product Principles.

---

# Design Goal

Users should understand what to do within three seconds.

The screen should answer only one question:

> What should I do today?

---

# Screen Structure

The Home screen consists of only four sections.

1. Greeting
2. Today's Mission
3. Health Score
4. AI Comment

No additional sections.

No charts.

No dashboards.

No tabs.

---

# Priority

Visual hierarchy must be:

Mission

↓

Score

↓

AI Comment

The Mission card should receive the greatest visual emphasis.

---

# Greeting

Display:

Good morning.

Good afternoon.

Good evening.

Greeting text should support today's mission.

Maximum two lines.

---

# Mission Card

The Mission card is the primary component of Health Pilot.

Display:

- Mission title
- Deadline
- Primary action button

Do NOT display:

- duplicate descriptions
- technical metrics
- star ratings
- impact score
- priority score

Example:

Today's Mission

Walk outside

Before 10:00

[ Complete ]

---

# Why Section

Hidden by default.

Collapsed.

Expandable.

Display only when requested.

Example:

▼ Why this mission?

After expansion:

- HRV below baseline
- Sleep debt increased
- Morning sunlight improves alertness

---

# Health Score

Purpose:

Show long-term progress.

Do not compete with today's mission.

The score card should be visually smaller than the Mission card.

---

# AI Comment

One short paragraph.

Maximum two sentences.

Avoid repeating the Mission.

Instead, provide encouragement or useful context.

---

# Visual Style

Minimal.

Calm.

Confident.

No unnecessary decoration.

Whitespace is part of the design.

---

# Color

Primary Accent

Blue

Success

Green

Warning

Orange

Critical

Red

Background

Light gray

Cards

White

---

# Typography

Mission title

Largest text on screen.

Deadline

Medium emphasis.

Body

Regular.

Secondary information

Muted.

---

# Buttons

One primary button only.

Label:

Complete

No secondary CTA.

---

# Animation

Keep subtle.

Mission completion:

- gentle scale
- fade
- checkmark

No confetti.

No gamification.

---

# Accessibility

Touch targets:

Minimum 44px.

Support Dynamic Type.

High contrast.

Keyboard accessible.

---

# Performance

Render immediately.

No loading animation for static demo data.

---

# Definition of Done

The Home screen satisfies all of the following:

- Only one Mission is displayed.
- Mission is the most visually prominent element.
- Health Score supports the Mission rather than competing with it.
- AI Comment is concise.
- Why section is collapsed by default.
- Layout follows Mission First.
- Screen feels calm and uncluttered.
- Users understand today's action within three seconds.
