# Product Principles

Health Pilot is not defined by its features.

It is defined by the principles behind every feature.

These principles guide every product decision.

---

# Simplicity Over Features

Every new feature adds complexity.

Only build features that make health decisions easier.

If a feature does not reduce cognitive load, do not build it.

---

# Action Over Information

Information alone rarely changes behavior.

Every feature should help users take meaningful action.

Health Pilot exists to turn information into decisions.

---

# Trust Over Engagement

Success is not measured by screen time.

Health Pilot should encourage users to leave the app and live healthier lives.

If the app is used less because people have already understood what to do, that is success.

---

# Long-Term Health Over Short-Term Metrics

Daily scores are useful.

Long-term habits matter more.

Optimize for sustainable behavior rather than temporary improvements.

---

# Privacy By Default

Health data is deeply personal.

Users should always understand what data is used, why it is used, and remain in control of it.

Trust is impossible without transparency.

---

# AI As A Copilot

AI assists.

AI explains.

AI recommends.

People remain responsible for decisions.

Health Pilot augments human judgment.

It never replaces it.

---

# Calm Technology

Technology should quietly support life.

It should not compete for attention.

Avoid unnecessary notifications, alerts, and interruptions.

The best interface is often the one that stays out of the way.

---

# Continuous Improvement

Health Pilot is never finished.

Every release should make the product:

- simpler
- clearer
- faster
- more helpful

Progress is measured by reduced effort for the user.

---

# Build For Years

Do not optimize for trends.

Do not optimize for demos.

Build decisions that will still feel right five years from now.

Timeless principles outlast fashionable features.

---

# Final Principle

Before building anything, ask one question:

**"Does this help people make better health decisions with less cognitive effort?"**

If the answer is no,

do not build it.
